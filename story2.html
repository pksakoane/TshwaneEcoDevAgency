Mayor calls for private investment
The Executive Mayor of Tshwane, Cllr Kgosientso Ramokgopa used the media and partners’ breakfast of the Tshwane International Trade and Infrastructure Investment Conference (TITIIC) to call for partnership between business and the City of Tshwane. The mayor highlighted that this cooperation is important in that the state alone cannot resolve challenges faced by society. He said it was important for the state to deliver on basic services and infrastructure which was an enabler for growth.

Ramokgopa told business representatives and media at the CSIR that the city will be issuing request for proposals soon for the rebuilding of both the Rooivaal and Pretoria West Power Stations to generate over 800 MW of electricity. To reach the target of the capacity required the City will also consider other sources of energy such as gas to fuel power stations.

“We are confident that we will make an announcement during the Budget Speech in May this year about the companies that will be appointed for the implementation of these projects” he said.

Furthermore, projects within the green economy sector have already been packaged and will also be showcased at the upcoming conference.

“Recently the City launched a project to auction land parcels as part of spatial configuration but also to raise capital for service delivery programme as our own resources are limited” said Ramokgopa.

The Mayor also highlighted that the City of Tshwane is also working on ensuring sustainable water supply to both the community and business since the City is not immune from possibilities of water supply shortages. Ramokgopa said that these infrastructure projects call for strategic partnership between business and government hence the City has initiated TITIIC.

For more information about the conference, contact Charlene Jansen at 011 463 5085 or visit the official conference website: www.titiic.com.
